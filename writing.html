No Smoking(2025) A desperate man's attempt at a fresh start ignites into violent and surreal encounters, forcing him to confront his past as his choices lead him down a path of fiery consequences and eternal damnation. Thriller, Drama, Surreal Feature
Summary The story centers on Creed Isaacs, a man in his fifties attempting to rebuild his life after a long period of homelessness and addiction. Creed secures a job at a Fuel City gas station owned by Garfield Gallard. This new beginning is fraught with challenges. Creed faces disdain from his younger coworker, Steven McGee, who resents Creed's presence. Despite Creed's efforts, he is caught smoking on the job, a direct violation of Fuel City's policy due to the old gas pumps, and is subsequently terminated by Gallard. Following his firing, Creed steals Steven's car from the Fuel City parking lot. In a state of desperation, Creed succumbs to his past and relapses with the help of an acquaintance, Siggy Meddlers. Throughout Creed's experiences, the narrative interweaves surreal sequences set in a black and white hospital waiting room. This waiting room is populated mostly by older individuals, and a bored man named Pete manages a line leading to double doors that emit a blinding white light. Individuals who pass through these doors are consumed by the light and make cryptic remarks about Creed's situation, suggesting he is in for a ‚Äúrude awakening.‚Äù These scenes reinforce a theme of purgatory, a cyclical repetition of events and judgment. Steven has encounters with Erin outside of work, initially marked by Steven's awkward attempts at connection. Their relationship develops into a somewhat contentious but also developing connection as they share experiences and vulnerabilities, such as discussing Steven's stolen car and their fathers. Steven attends a party at Gallard's mansion while Gallard is away. At the party, Steven encounters Siggy, has a bizarre encounter with a raccoon in an upstairs bathroom, and inadvertently unleashes Howard, a giant crane, causing chaos. The narrative reaches a violent climax when Creed returns to Fuel City, armed with a black pistol he stole from Siggy. In a heated confrontation, fueled by his anger and desperation, Creed shoots Gallard. This act triggers a chaotic escape in Steven's stolen car, with Erin unexpectedly joining him in the backseat, injured. Their escape is short-lived and ends abruptly as Steven's car is engulfed in orange light. Following this fiery conclusion, the narrative returns to the hospital waiting room for Creed, reinforcing the sense of an inescapable situation. He then wakes up alone in Steven's car, stripped of the pistol, his money, cigarettes, and even the car keys. The grim consequences of his actions become apparent as Creed sees Fuel City engulfed in flames. Ultimately, Creed is apprehended by the police at the train station, seemingly after being set up by his brother Mike, who expresses a sorrowful farewell before the arrest. Meanwhile, in the aftermath of the chaos, Steven and Erin find a moment of quiet connection, suggesting a potential shift in their relationship amidst the turmoil.
